<p><p>Ultra-pasteurized heavy cream is a type of cream that has been heated to a higher temperature than regular pasteurized cream in order to extend its shelf life. This process involves heating the cream to a temperature of at least 280°F for 2 seconds, compared to traditional pasteurization which heats the cream to 165°F for 15 seconds. Ultra-pasteurized heavy cream is often preferred by consumers and food manufacturers for its longer shelf life and ability to withstand higher temperatures without curdling.</p><p>The ultra-pasteurized heavy cream market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 9.9% during the forecast period.
