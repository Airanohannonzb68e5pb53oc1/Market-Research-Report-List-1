<p><p>Wire wound RF inductors are widely used in low frequency and high frequency applications. Low frequency RF inductors are designed for applications in the range of 1 kHz to 1 MHz, while high frequency RF inductors are used for applications above 1 MHz. Low frequency inductors typically have larger physical dimensions and higher inductance values, while high frequency inductors are compact and offer lower inductance values. Both types of inductors are essential components in radio frequency circuits for maintaining signal integrity and filtering unwanted frequencies.</p></p>
