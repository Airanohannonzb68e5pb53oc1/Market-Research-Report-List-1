<p><p>Agricultural biofungicides are natural or organic products used to control fungal diseases in crops. The market is segmented into different types such as Trichoderma, Bacillus, Pseudomonas, Streptomyces, and others. Trichoderma is a common biofungicide that helps in suppressing pathogens. Bacillus-based biofungicides produce antibiotics that inhibit fungal growth. Pseudomonas biofungicides compete with harmful pathogens for nutrients. Streptomyces biofungicides produce enzymes that break down fungal cell walls. Other biofungicides work through various mechanisms to protect crops from fungal diseases.</p></p>

Foliar treatment involves spraying biofungicides onto the leaves of plants to prevent fungal growth. Seed treatment involves coating seeds with biofungicides before planting to protect them from soil-borne pathogens.
