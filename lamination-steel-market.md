<p><p>Lamination steel, also known as silicon steel, is a type of steel specifically designed for use as core material in electric equipment such as transformers, motors, and generators. It is characterized by its high magnetic permeability and low core loss, making it ideal for applications requiring efficient energy conversion. Lamination steel is typically coated with a thin layer of insulating oxide to reduce eddy current losses.</p><p>The global lamination steel market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 4.7% during the forecast period.
