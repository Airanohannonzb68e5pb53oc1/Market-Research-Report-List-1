<p><p>Hardware-assisted verification systems come in various types, including hardware emulation, FPGA prototyping, and others. Hardware emulation involves using specialized hardware to mimic the behavior of a system or component for testing purposes. FPGA prototyping utilizes field-programmable gate arrays to create a physical prototype of a design for testing. Other types of hardware-assisted verification systems may include simulation acceleration, in-circuit emulation, and virtual platforms. These technologies are essential for verifying the functionality and performance of complex hardware designs before production.</p></p>
