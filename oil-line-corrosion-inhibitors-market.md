<p><p>Oil line corrosion inhibitors are chemicals used to protect pipelines from corrosion caused by the transportation of oil and gas. The market for these inhibitors can be categorized into two main types: organic inhibitors and inorganic inhibitors. </p><p>Organic inhibitors are derived from carbon-based compounds and work by forming a protective film over the metal surface, preventing corrosion. Inorganic inhibitors, on the other hand, are typically metal salts that function by reacting with the metal surface to create a protective barrier against corrosion.</p></p>
