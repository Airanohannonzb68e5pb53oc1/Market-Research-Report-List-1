<p><p>Next-generation battery market types include graphene battery, lithium sulfur battery, sodium carbon dioxide battery, lithium air battery, and other emerging technologies. Graphene batteries use graphene, a single layer of carbon atoms, to enhance energy storage and improve charging capabilities. Lithium sulfur batteries offer a higher energy density and longer lifespan compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. Sodium carbon dioxide batteries use sodium ions and carbon dioxide as the working elements, offering a cost-effective and environmentally friendly alternative. Lithium air batteries have a higher energy density, allowing for longer-lasting power. Other emerging technologies, such as solid-state batteries and flow batteries, are also being explored for their potential in the next-generation battery market.</p></p>
