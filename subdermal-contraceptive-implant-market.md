<p><p>A subdermal contraceptive implant is a small rod-shaped device that is inserted under the skin of a woman's upper arm to prevent pregnancy. It releases a progestin hormone that works to prevent ovulation, thicken cervical mucus to block sperm, and thin the uterine lining to prevent implantation of a fertilized egg. This form of contraception is highly effective and can last for up to three to five years before needing to be replaced.</p><p>The Subdermal Contraceptive Implant Market is experiencing significant growth, with a forecasted CAGR of 14.3% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to factors such as increasing awareness about contraception, rising prevalence of unintended pregnancies, and the convenience and effectiveness of subdermal implants compared to other forms of birth control. Additionally, advancements in technology and research in the field of contraceptive implants are driving market growth.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the Subdermal Contraceptive Implant Market include the development of long-acting reversible contraceptive methods, increasing adoption of hormonal implants among women of reproductive age, and expanding access to contraception in developing countries. The market is expected to continue growing as more women opt for long-term and highly effective birth control options.</p></p>
