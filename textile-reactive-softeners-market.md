<p><p>Textile Reactive Softeners are categorized into three main types based on their chemical properties: Cationic Softeners, Anionic Softeners, and Non-Ionic Softeners. </p><p>1. Cationic Softeners are positively charged molecules that are attracted to the negatively charged surface of textiles, providing a soft and smooth feel.</p><p>2. Anionic Softeners are negatively charged molecules that work well on synthetic fibers, adding softness and reducing static.</p><p>3. Non-Ionic Softeners are neutral molecules that are versatile in their application and are compatible with a wide range of fabrics.</p></p>
