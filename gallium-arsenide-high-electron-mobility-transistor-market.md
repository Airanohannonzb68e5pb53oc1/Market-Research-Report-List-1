<p><p>Gallium Arsenide High Electron Mobility Transistors (GaAs HEMTs) are semiconductor devices that come in two main types: enhanced and depletion. Enhanced mode GaAs HEMTs operate with a positive gate voltage to increase electron density and enhance conductivity, making them suitable for high-frequency applications. Depletion mode GaAs HEMTs operate with a negative gate voltage to reduce electron density and decrease conductivity, making them suitable for low-power applications that require high linearity and power efficiency. Both types offer unique advantages and applications in the field of high-speed electronics.</p></p>
