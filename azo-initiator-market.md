<p><p>Azo-initiators are classified into two main market types based on their solubility: oil solubility and water solubility. Oil soluble azo-initiators are widely used in the polymer industry for initiating radical polymerization reactions in oil-based systems. On the other hand, water soluble azo-initiators are preferred in aqueous systems for their ease of use and compatibility with water-based formulations. Both types of azo-initiators play a crucial role in initiating polymerization reactions in their respective solvents.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Bulk Polymerization</li><li>Suspension Polymerization</li><li>Solution Polymerization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Azo-initiators are widely used in bulk polymerization, suspension polymerization, and solution polymerization processes. In bulk polymerization, azo-initiators are typically used to initiate the polymerization reaction in a homogeneous mixture of monomers. In suspension polymerization, they are used to initiate the polymerization reaction in a heterogeneous mixture of monomers and polymer beads. In solution polymerization, azo-initiators are used to start the polymerization reaction in a solution of monomers and solvents. These applications of azo-initiators play a crucial role in the production of various polymers for different industries.</p></p>
