<p><p>Gas sensors for semiconductors are classified into two main types: resistive and non-resistive. Resistive gas sensors change their resistance in response to the presence of a particular gas, making them sensitive and reliable. Non-resistive gas sensors, on the other hand, operate based on other principles such as the generation of an electric field or the measurement of light absorption. Both types of sensors have their own unique advantages and are used in various applications within the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
